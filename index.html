About the Daily Bruin Print Archive

The Daily Bruin has served the UCLA community as the campus' premier student-run newspaper since 1919, and its predecessors date back to 1912. On this site you can read every issue of the Daily Bruin from 1915 to present.

The digital archives of the Daily Bruin from September 1915 to December 2002 on this site were made possible by the UCLA Library and are hosted by the Internet Archive. The more recent papers, starting in January 2003, are compiled from Daily Bruin staff archives.

This website is updated nightly with each morning's newspaper. The Bruin prints five times per week during Week 1 through Week 9 of the UCLA quarter, on Monday and Thursday of Week 10, and on Monday of Finals Week. It also prints Mondays during UCLA's summer sessions.

Bound archives of the Daily Bruin are available upon request, and may be viewed in the Bruin's offices at 118 Kerckhoff Hall, UCLA. Please call (310) 825-9898 to schedule an appointment to access the bound archives.

How to navigate this site

Use the left and right arrows, or your left and right arrow keys, to switch between pages in the archives. You may also select a specific page by changing the number at the end of this site's web address.

In order to search by date, select a month and year from the drop-down menus in the upper right corner of the site, and press the "Go" button. Dates are approximate.

For dates between 1915 and 2002, some pages may be rotated. You may use the zoom and rotate buttons at the bottom of the screen to manipulate any page between 1915 and 2002. An image may be download by right-clicking and saving as a .jpg file.

For dates more recent than 2003, you will then select a month and day in order to find the appropriate page.

Search functionality is not yet available on this site.

Historical Highlights

1910s

  • 1915: Newspaper name becomes Normal Outlook
  • 1919: Campus becomes a branch of the University of California; newspaper name becomes Cub Californian

1920s

  • 1924: Newspaper name becomes California Grizzly
  • 1926: Newspaper name becomes Daily Bruin
  • 1927: First structure at UCLA's Westwood campus, a bridge across what is now Dickson Court, opens
  • 1929: New UCLA campus opens in Westwood

1930s

  • 1931: Kerckhoff Hall, the original student union building, is dedicated
  • 1931: Ernest C. Moore becomes provost
  • 1932: UCLA experiences its first snowfall
  • 1932: Albert Einstein speaks in Royce Hall
  • 1937: Earle R. Hedrick becomes provost

1940s

  • 1941: Japanese forces attack Pearl Harbor
  • 1942: UCLA and USC compete to sell war bonds during crosstown football game
  • 1945: Clarence A. Dykstra becomes provost
  • 1945: End of World War II
  • 1948: John Wooden hired as basketball coach

1950s

  • 1950: UCLA wins its first NCAA national team title, in tennis
  • 1950: UCLA alumnus Ralph Bunche becomes the first Bruin to win the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1952: Raymond B. Allen becomes chancellor
  • 1954: UCLA beats USC, 34-0, in crosstown football game
  • 1959: Vern O. Knudsen becomes chancellor
  • 1959: Dedication of Dykstra Hall, first dorm building on the Hill

1960s

  • 1960: Franklin D. Murphy becomes chancellor
  • 1961: New student union building, later named Ackerman Union, opens
  • 1965: Martin Luther King, Jr. speaks at the bottom of Janss Steps
  • 1965: First basketball game played in Pauley Pavilion
  • 1968: Charles E. Young becomes chancellor
  • 1969: Leonard Kleinrock sends the first message on the ARPANET; the Internet is born
  • 1969: First men land on the moon
  • 1969: Philosophy professor Angela Davis dismissed

1970s

  • 1972: Ronald Reagan writes critical op-ed about The Bruin's coverage
  • 1974: UCLA elevates women’s athletics programs to full status
  • 1975: John Wooden announces retirement

1980s

  • 1984: UCLA hosts Olympic Village and gymnastics and tennis events during the 1984 Olympic Games
  • 1984: Bruin Bear statue unveiled

1990s

  • 1994: Northridge earthquake occurs
  • 1994: President Bill Clinton speaks at commencement ceremony
  • 1997: Albert Carnesale becomes chancellor

2000s

  • 2001: September 11 attacks take place
  • 2002: David Geffen donates $200 million to UCLA School of Medicine
  • 2006: Norman Abrams becomes acting chancellor
  • 2006: Student stunned with taser by university police in Powell Library
  • 2007: Gene D. Block becomes chancellor
  • 2007: UCLA becomes first university to win 100 NCAA team championships
  • 2008: Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center opens
  • 2008: First issue of prime | by the Daily Bruin is published

2010s

  • 2010: John Wooden dies
  • 2011: UCLA receives $200 million gift from the Lincy Foundation
  • 2011: UCLA student Alexandra Wallace posts “Asians in the Library” video
  • 2012: Renovated Pauley Pavilion opens to the public
  • 2014: Sunset Boulevard water main breaks, flooding central campus
  • 2016: Murder-suicide takes place in Engineering IV building
